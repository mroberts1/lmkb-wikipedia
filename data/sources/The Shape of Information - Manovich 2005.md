# The Shape of Information

## Summary

A short programmatic essay introducing the concept of **info-aesthetics** — a project to scan contemporary culture for emerging aesthetics and cultural forms specific to global information society. Manovich asks: has the shift from form to information flows been accompanied by new vocabularies of forms, new aesthetic sensibilities, and new iconologies?

## Key Arguments

- **"Information" contains "form"**: Despite living in "information society," we remain form-creating species. We need to design forms for ourselves and for information.
- **Info-aesthetics defined**: Not a single style but various contemporary cultural practices best understood as responses to the priorities of information society — making sense of information, working with it, producing knowledge from it.
- **Information visualization**: The most obvious example — designers create forms that represent quantitative data, helping us understand it. These forms are related neither to the human form (classical art) nor abstracted from it (modern art).
- **New information powers**: Architects (Koolhaas, Hadid, UN Studio), media artists (Fujihata's Field Works), and filmmakers (Timecode, Russian Arc) all utilize capacities to record, index, and manipulate exponentially larger amounts of media.
- **"Life is data, progress is optional"**: If life has indeed become data, what new forms will help us make sense of it?
