# Filters, Plug-ins, and Menus

## Summary

An expanded version of the "creation to selection" argument from [[Zeuxis meets RealityEngine - Manovich 1996]], combined with the "cinema as interface" thesis from [[Cinema as Cultural Interface - Manovich 1997]]. Manovich argues that digital tools encode existing cultural norms as defaults, making it difficult for users to imagine alternatives — with cinema's language being the most consequential default being embedded in software.

## Key Arguments

- **From creation to selection**: Digital culture replaces authentic creation with selection from menus. The Theremin (1920) — modifying an existing signal — established the logic; synthesizers, paint programs, and 3D libraries all follow the same pattern. "You push the button, we create your world."
- **Cinema as interface**: Cinematic elements (mobile camera, rectangular frame, editing conventions) are progressively encoded into software and hardware. Camera controls built into the N64 joypad represent a historic moment: "directing the virtual camera becomes as important as controlling the hero's actions."
- **Automating culture**: Once cultural codes are implemented in low-level software and hardware, they cease to be seen as choices and become unquestionable defaults. Linear perspective → mobile camera → cinematography conventions → digital personas → narrative structures.
- **VRML as cinematic interface**: VRML worlds feature pre-computed viewpoints and dolly-like camera movements — "nature is firmly subsumed under culture."
- **Resisting defaults**: Manovich's own *Reality Generator*, Art+Com's *Invisible Shape of Things Past*, and Waliczky's custom perspective films each refuse the universalization of cinematic vision.
