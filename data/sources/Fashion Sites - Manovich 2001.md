# Fashion Sites

## Summary

A brief, provocative column arguing that fashion has become the art form most concerned with reimagining the human figure — a role that visual art abandoned in the 20th century. Manovich reviews several fashion websites (firstview.com, Costume National, Louis Vuitton, Nike iD) as examples of web design at its best and as windows into the future of personalization.

## Key Arguments

- **Fashion as what art is not**: Fashion is concerned with beauty, aware of its deep history, semiotically layered, and constrained by the human figure. Art abandoned portraying humans; fashion continuously reinvents them.
- **Fashion as imaginary biology**: Fashion's avant-garde wing asks "what else a human being could have been?" — a playful Darwinian thought experiment.
- **Nike iD as future**: Nike's shoe customization site — choose colors, add a personal ID — represents personalization moving from new media (customized paths, homepages) into manufacturing. "Self-expression is at the heart of human nature."
- **Interactivity as sensual**: The Louis Vuitton site proves that "interactivity itself can be as sensual as the best underwear."
