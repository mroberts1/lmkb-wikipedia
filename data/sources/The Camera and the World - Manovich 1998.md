# The Camera and the World

## Summary

An essay on Tamás Waliczky's aesthetic research, arguing that his work systematically maps the space where new ways to structure the virtual world and new ways to see it meet. Waliczky is characterized as a maker of "virtual documentaries" — each work creates a world structured in a unique way, then documents it with a camera that is inseparable from the world itself.

## Key Arguments

- **Camera-world fusion**: In Waliczky's works, the virtual camera and the virtual world function as parts of a single gestalt. The camera is not just a tool of perception but of epistemology — without its operations, the structure of the world would remain hidden.
- **Post-cinematic cosmologies**: Each work establishes a unique cosmology whose structure can only be revealed by actions of a virtual camera. "Landscape" freezes time to create a new world; "Sculptures" reveals time-sculptures through camera movement; "Focus" hands camera controls to the viewer.
- **Refusing defaults**: Waliczky openly refuses the default one-point perspective imposed by computer software, creating custom 3D software for alternative perspectival systems (water-drop, cylindrical, reverse).
- **Against cinematic universalization**: Computer culture translates cinematic perception into a decontextualized toolbox divorced from material embodiment. Waliczky's works refuse this separation, reuniting perception and material reality.
