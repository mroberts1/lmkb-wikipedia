# Zeuxis meets RealityEngine: Digital Realism and Virtual Worlds

## Summary

A major essay examining four distinctive features of realism in virtual worlds: realism as commodity, the shift from creation to selection, "Brecht as hardware" (the built-in self-deconstruction of digital illusion), and the "aggregate" (non-systematic) nature of computer-generated space. Manovich uses art-historical categories (Riegl, Panofsky, Wölfflin) to argue that virtual spaces have not even reached their "Renaissance" yet.

## Key Arguments

- **Realism as commodity**: In digital media, all dimensions affecting the reality effect (resolution, color depth, polygon count, frame rate) are quantifiable — and therefore priceable. "Realism has become a commodity." Future virtual worlds may charge users for visual quality, extending the logic of pornography to all culture. Snow Crash's tiered avatar quality as prophecy.
- **From creation to selection**: Digital culture replaces artistic creation from scratch with assembly from ready-made parts (3D model libraries, Photoshop filters, pre-assembled animations). "You push the button, we create your world." Interactive art's "co-authorship" is really just selecting from pre-existing options. This parallels the consumer logic of modern life.
- **Brecht as hardware**: Virtual worlds constantly oscillate between illusion and its disruption — loading screens, level-of-detail switching, QuickTime VR pixelation. Brecht's distanciation strategy is embedded in the technology itself. "Meta-realism" replaces old totalizing realism, matching how contemporary ideology "continuously and skillfully deconstructs itself."
- **Aggregate space**: Despite using linear perspective, virtual worlds are actually collections of disconnected objects, not coherent spatial environments (Panofsky's "aggregate" vs. "systematic" space). Using Riegl's categories: CG space is "haptic" not "optic." VRML worlds lack *prostranstvennaya sreda* (space-medium). "There is no space in cyberspace." The Web itself is aggregate — hyperlinked files without overall perspective.
