# The Language of New Media (article)

## Summary

A programmatic outline for what would become Manovich's landmark book *The Language of New Media* (2001). The article proposes four critical categories for analyzing new media — **interface**, **database**, **navigation**, and **spatialization** — and identifies two key genres of computer culture: the database and navigable space.

## Key Arguments

- **Need for a new critical language**: Technical terms like "website" are insufficient; traditional cultural concepts (image, narrative, montage) need redefinition for computer culture. The critical language must correlate older cultural/theoretical concepts with the concepts of digital computing.
- **Four categories**: Interface (where does it end and "content" begin?), Database (origins, ideology, aesthetics of the database form), Spatialization (why is space being privileged in computer culture?), Navigation (how does it relate to viewing, reading, identifying?).
- **Two key genres**: Database and navigable space — creation in new media means either constructing an interface to a multimedia database or defining a navigation method through spatialized representations.
- **Vertov as anticipation**: *Man with a Movie Camera* anticipates every trend of 1990s new media, particularly its database structure and navigation through space.
- **Greenaway and database cinema**: Like Vertov, Greenaway reconciles database and narrative — his films foreground the interface conventions of the computer.
- **Waliczky's custom perspectives**: Refuses the default one-point perspective imposed by software, creating alternative spatial systems.
