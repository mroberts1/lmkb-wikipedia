# Jump over Proust

## Summary

Manovich calls for multimedia writing that can represent human subjectivity and the thinking process with greater fidelity than literature or cinema have achieved. He looks to Eisenstein, Marker, and especially Godard as models, and argues that software concepts (relational databases, control structures, hierarchical file systems) are already better "artistic visions of our inner life" than most multimedia art.

## Key Arguments

- **The challenge**: How to use new representational capacities of the computer to take the user "inside" the thinking process of another subject — not just as a "co-author" but into someone's mental space.
- **Godard as model**: *Histoires du Cinéma* uses superimposed images that fade in/out (like ideas floating in consciousness) and oscillation between two images (representing associative movement from one idea to another).
- **Cinema's failure**: Despite Münsterberg's 1916 analysis mapping cinematic techniques to mental functions, cinema mostly followed 19th-century novel conventions rather than matching the achievement of Joyce, Proust, Nabokov.
- **Software as better mirror**: "Software tools themselves are more revolutionary than multimedia applications they are used to design." Relational databases, pointers, control structures, object-oriented programming — these point toward richer representations of mind than hyperlinked pages.
- **Human thinking ≈ executing program**: "Human thinking is certainly more like a computer program under execution ... than a set of pages linked by hyperlinks."
