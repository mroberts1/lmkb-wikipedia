# Virtual Worlds: Report from Los Angeles

## Summary

A brief report on the trend toward spatialization in computer culture — from VRML and chat environments to scientific visualization and computer games. Manovich notes that computer culture is increasingly mapping all information, narrative, and even time onto 3D space, and asks what the visual aesthetics of spatialized cyberspace will be. The piece ends without resolving this question.

## Key Arguments

- **Spatialization as mega-trend**: HCI moving from 2D desktops to 3D rooms/cities; scientific visualization spatializing data; computer games equating narrative with movement through space.
- **SIMNET → Battletech**: Military collaborative VR (SIMNET) directly converted into civilian entertainment (Battletech Center), collapsing the boundary between military training and play.
- **VRML**: Conceived as spatial equivalent of the Web, eventually subsuming all other media within a 3D navigable environment.
- **Computer animation as "ultimate road movie"**: Camera flies through space with no narrative goal — navigation as sufficient content.
