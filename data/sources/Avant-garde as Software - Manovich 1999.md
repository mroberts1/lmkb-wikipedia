# Avant-garde as Software

## Summary

A major essay arguing that the 1920s avant-garde's communication techniques became embedded in computer software, while the computer age's truly new avant-garde operates not on the level of forms but of information access and manipulation. Manovich traces four specific transformations (visual atomism → discrete data, montage → windows, New Typography → GUI, New Vision → 3D visualization) and theorizes "meta-media society" where the key cultural task shifts from recording reality to processing accumulated media.

## Key Arguments

- **1920s as key decade**: All fundamental modern visual communication techniques were invented in the 1920s (montage, collage, classical film language, surrealism, modern graphic design, sex appeal in advertising). No fundamentally new approaches emerged after — only intensification and recombination.
- **Four transformations**:
  1. **Visual atomism → discrete ontology**: The avant-garde idea that complex messages can be built from simple elements with known psychological effects becomes the technological basis of all digital media (pixels, polygons, HTML elements, hyperlinks).
  2. **Montage → windows**: Kay's overlapping windows (1969) synthesize temporal montage (switching between windows) and montage-within-a-shot (multiple windows visible simultaneously), solving the cognitive overload Vertov hit with superimposition.
  3. **New Typography → GUI**: Tschichold's clarity principle ("the essence of the New Typography is clarity") becomes the graphical style of Windows/Mac OS. New Typography's hierarchical organization becomes meta-design — tools for users to organize information themselves (nested folders, zoom, outline views).
  4. **New Vision → 3D visualization**: Moholy-Nagy and Rodchenko's defamiliarizing viewpoints become standard operations — zoom, pan, rotate, negate. "Defamiliarization now involves simply a movement of a computer mouse."
- **Meta-media society**: After ~100 years of accumulating media records, society shifts from recording reality to processing accumulated media. Cinema foregrounded perception; computer foregrounds memory. "Post-modernism" is one effect; the absence of new forms in new media is another.
- **The new avant-garde**: Not new forms but new ways to access and manipulate information — databases, search engines, data mining, visualization, simulation, hypermedia. "From 'a man with a movie camera' to a user with a search engine."
- **Double meaning**: "The avant-garde becomes software" means both (1) software codifies and naturalizes old avant-garde techniques, and (2) software's new techniques of working with media represent the new avant-garde.
