# Automation of Sight: From Photography to Computer Vision

## Summary

An expanded and more focused version of the perspective-automation argument from earlier essays. Manovich traces the automation of perspectival sight from Dürer's machines through photography to 3D computer graphics and computer vision, arguing that computer vision simultaneously represents the apotheosis of perspective and the beginning of its retreat — as researchers discover that perspectival images are too ambiguous for reliable 3D reconstruction and turn to alternative sensing technologies.

## Key Arguments

- **Photography automated one use of perspective** (imaging physical reality) but not others (geometric modeling, visualization of imagined objects). Computers automated the rest.
- **Roberts' dual achievement (1963-65)**: His papers simultaneously founded 3D computer graphics AND computer vision — "automation of imaging and of sight."
- **Computer vision as Cold War product**: CIA/NSA funded early AI projects; mechanical translation of Soviet communications led to pattern recognition; NPIC created to process overwhelming volume of satellite imagery.
- **The retreat of perspective**: Initially, researchers believed they could reconstruct 3D scenes from a single perspectival image. But perspective proved too ambiguous (multiple spatial layouts produce the same 2D image). Solutions: stereo vision (multiple images), range finders (bypassing perspective entirely). "Perspective becomes a limit to the final automation of sight."
- **Vision reduced to action**: In computer vision, "understanding a scene" means being able to act on it — move objects, destroy targets. Pragmatism (James, Pierce) applied to cognition.
