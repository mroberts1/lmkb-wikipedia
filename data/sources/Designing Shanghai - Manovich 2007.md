# Designing Shanghai, or Why East Is the New West

## Summary

A long observational essay based on Manovich's extended stays in Shanghai (2005-2006) arguing that Asia — and Shanghai in particular — has embraced contemporary design more widely and inventively than the West. He examines creative industry hubs (Bridge 8, X2, New Factories), compares consumer spaces across Asia, Europe, and North America, and proposes that "East is the new West" because these countries built their consumer infrastructure during the design/branding era.

## Key Arguments

- **Asia ahead in design**: New consumer spaces in Shanghai, Bangkok, and Singapore feature better design than comparable spaces in North America or Europe — better materials, finishes, ambience, and coordination. "Fast food places in Shanghai may be mistaken for fancy restaurants."
- **Timing explanation**: Western spaces were mostly built before the design/branding era (pre-1996). Asian and Eastern European countries built new consumer spaces in the 2000s, when design sensibility was already mainstream. Younger decision-makers and fresher infrastructure.
- **Different aesthetics**: Western space design projects dignity and middle-age sophistication (grey, black, white). Asian aesthetics speaks of youth, dynamism, and energy (bright colors, dynamic patterns, large photographic images).
- **Creative industry hubs**: Shanghai's Bridge 8, X2, and New Factories combine professional design workspaces with consumer spaces (cafes, restaurants, clubs, galleries). This integration of production and consumption — "Wallpaper meets Frame" — is unprecedented.
- **Three eras of Chinese consumer culture**: (1) Non-existing (Mao era); (2) Western imports assembled in China; (3) China developing its own design/creative industries for export.
- **Airport trays as design index**: San Diego (unattractive) → Amsterdam (bright but cheap) → Oslo (sophisticated Scandinavian) → Singapore Changi (avant-garde design statement). Design quality inversely correlates with Western assumptions about development.
- **"Designed in Shanghai. Assembled in the US"**: Manovich imagines a 25-year future where Asia dominates knowledge and creative economies, with North America reduced to manufacturing.
