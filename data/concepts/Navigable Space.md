# Navigable Space

## Definition

One of the two key cultural forms of the computer age (alongside [[Database as Symbolic Form]]). Navigable space is the experience of moving through a 3D virtual environment — from Doom to VRML worlds to Google Earth — which Manovich argues represents a genuinely new cultural form with its own aesthetics and poetics.

## Core Thesis

While cinema organized culture through montage (editing in time), computer culture increasingly organizes experience through navigation in space. The user's movement through space becomes the primary narrative mechanism. This represents a shift from "time-based" to "space-based" cultural forms.

## Key Principles

- **Space as medium**: In new media, space is not just a container for action but the primary aesthetic and narrative material — the experience *is* the navigation
- **Computer space vs. physical space**: Computer spaces obey different rules — they can be non-Euclidean, discontinuous, and infinitely extensible. Manovich traces this genealogy through Dziga Vertov's constructed spaces and El Lissitzky's abstract environments
- **Flâneur and explorer**: The user of navigable space inherits roles from 19th-century urban culture (Benjamin's flâneur) and from exploration/colonization narratives
- **Two traditions**: (1) Transparent navigation (immersive environments like Myst) vs. (2) self-conscious navigation that foregrounds the interface and the act of moving

## Evolution of the Concept

### Foundational mapping (1993-1995)
In [[Mapping of Space - Manovich 1993]], Manovich begins analyzing how computer mapping creates new spatial representations. In [[Externalization of Psyche - Manovich 1995]], he connects virtual space to psychological interiority — navigating virtual worlds as navigating mental landscapes.

### Full theorization (1998)
In [[Navigable Space - Manovich 1998]], Manovich provides the comprehensive theory: computer space as a new cultural form with its own poetics, analyzed through Doom, Myst, and VRML worlds. He traces the genealogy from Baroque spatial arts through cinema to virtual environments.

### Cultural interface (1997)
In [[Cinema as Cultural Interface - Manovich 1997]], navigation is positioned as one of three key cultural interface traditions (alongside cinema and the printed page) that shape how we interact with cultural data.

### Augmented space (2002)
In [[Poetics of Augmented Space - Manovich 2002]], the concept evolves from virtual space to physical space overlaid with data — augmented reality, video surveillance, electronic displays. Navigation now occurs in hybrid physical-digital spaces.

## Relation to Other Concepts

- [[Database as Symbolic Form]] — The paradigmatic complement: database provides the data, navigation provides the trajectory
- [[Cultural Interface]] — Navigation as one mode of interfacing with cultural data
- [[Augmented Space]] — The evolution from purely virtual navigable space to data-enhanced physical space
