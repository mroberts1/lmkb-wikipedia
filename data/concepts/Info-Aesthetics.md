# Info-Aesthetics

## Definition

Manovich's proposed theoretical program connecting the study of information (its structures, organization, access patterns) to aesthetics (form, style, beauty, experience). Info-aesthetics asks: what does information *look like*, *feel like*, and how do the formal properties of information systems produce aesthetic experiences?

## Core Thesis

Information is not formless data awaiting visualization — it has inherent aesthetic properties related to its structure, density, organization, redundancy, and access patterns. A new aesthetics is needed that can account for how we experience information environments (interfaces, databases, search engines, visualizations) as aesthetic phenomena. This program bridges information theory (Shannon), design, and cultural theory.

## Key Principles

- **Form = information structure**: The visual form of information (how a database is visualized, how an interface organizes content) is itself an aesthetic statement. The distinction between "information" and "its representation" collapses
- **Scale as aesthetic variable**: The amount of information changes the aesthetic experience — a 4K image is not just a higher-resolution version of SD but a qualitatively different aesthetic object
- **Beyond transparency**: The "invisible interface" ideology (information should be delivered without distortion) is replaced by recognizing that interface and presentation are constitutive of the aesthetic experience
- **Connecting Tufte and Greenberg**: Info-aesthetics bridges the world of information design (Tufte's clarity principles) and the world of art criticism (Greenberg's formalism) — two traditions that rarely interact

## Evolution of the Concept

### First formulation (2005)
In [[The Shape of Information - Manovich 2005]], Manovich outlines the info-aesthetics program and calls for connecting information visualization research to aesthetic theory.

### Draft book introduction (2005)
In [[Introduction to Info-Aesthetics - Manovich 2005]], Manovich provides the most detailed treatment — proposing a new critical vocabulary around information form, connecting it to scale, complexity, and the aesthetics of software interfaces.

### Aesthetic events (2007)
In [[Information as an Aesthetic Event - Manovich 2007]], Manovich shows info-aesthetics in action: analyzing how OS X Aqua, the LG Chocolate phone, and iPhone treat information interaction as an explicitly aesthetic *event* rather than a transparent delivery mechanism.
