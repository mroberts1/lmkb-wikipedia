# Post-media Aesthetics

## Definition

The aesthetic condition that arises when the computer dissolves the boundaries between previously distinct media. If every medium is now simulated in software, then medium-specific aesthetics (Greenberg's modernist program) no longer applies. Manovich explores what aesthetic theory and practice look like after the concept of "a medium" loses its explanatory power.

## Core Thesis

The computer has made all media equivalent at the level of digital code. Photography, film, painting, typography, sound, and 3D modeling all become data manipulable by the same algorithms. This doesn't mean media differences disappear — they persist as conventions, interface metaphors, and cultural habits — but the *ontological* basis for medium specificity is gone. Aesthetics must now account for hybrid, cross-media, and software-native forms.

## Key Principles

- **Software as universal solvent**: When all media exist as data in the same computational environment, the material basis for medium-specific aesthetics dissolves
- **From medium to software**: Instead of asking "what is unique to photography?" or "what is unique to cinema?", post-media aesthetics asks "what does this software enable?" — the tool replaces the medium as the unit of analysis
- **Three types of remix** (from Korean Edition): Different media combining in single objects, new species of media, and hybrid aesthetics combining strategies from different traditions — all are post-media phenomena
- **Not post-art**: Post-media doesn't mean art is over — it means the organizing principle of discrete media no longer structures artistic practice
